scene ii a forest near rome horn and cry of hound heard enter titu andronicu with hunter c marcu luciu quintu and martiu titu andronicu the hunt is up the morn is bright and grey the field are fragrant and the wood are green uncouple here and let us make a bay and wake the emperor and his lovely bride and rouse the prince and ring a hunter s peal that all the court may echo with the noise son let it be your charge as it is ours to attend the emperor s person careful i have been trouble in my sleep this night but dawn day new comfort hath inspired a cry of hound and horn wind in a peal enter saturninu tamora bassianu lavinia demetriu chiron and attendant many good morrow to your majesty madam to you as many and as good i promise your grace a hunter s peal saturninu and you have rung it lusty my lord somewhat too early for new married ladies bassianu lavinia how say you lavinia i say no i have been broad awake two hour and more saturninu come on then horse and chariot let us have and to our sport to tamora madam now shall ye see our roman hunting marcu andronicu i have dogs my lord will rouse the proudest panther in the chase and climb the highest promontory top titu andronicu and i have horse will follow where the game make way and run like swallow o er the plain demetriu chiron we hunt not we with horse nor hound but hope to pluck a dainty doe to ground exeunt
