scene iv a camp in wales enter earl of salisbury and a welsh captain captain my lord of salisbury we have stay d ten days and hardly kept our countrymen together and yet we hear no tidings from the king therefore we will disperse ourselves farewell earl of salisbury stay yet another day thou trusty welshman the king reposeth all his confidence in thee captain  tis thought the king is dead we will not stay the bay tree in our country are all wither d and meteor fright the fixed stars of heaven the pale face moon looks bloody on the earth and lean look d prophets whisper fearful change rich men look sad and ruffian dance and leap the one in fear to lose what they enjoy the other to enjoy by rage and war these sign forerun the death or fall of kings farewell our countrymen are gone and flee as well assured richard their king is dead exit earl of salisbury ah richard with the eyes of heavy mind i see thy glory like a shoot star fall to the base earth from the firmament thy sun set weeping in the lowly west witness storm to come woe and unrest thy friends are flee to wait upon thy foe and cross to thy good all fortune go exit
