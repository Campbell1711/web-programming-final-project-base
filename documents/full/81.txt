scene iii field near st alban s alarum retreat enter york richard warwick and soldier with drum and colours york of salisbury who can report of him that winter lion who in rage forget age contusion and all brush of time and like a gallant in the brow of youth repair him with occasion this happy day is not itself nor have we won one foot if salisbury be lost richard my noble father three times to day i holp him to his horse three times bestrid him thrice i led him off persuade him from any further act but still where danger was still there i met him and like rich hangings in a homely house so was his will in his old feeble body but noble as he is look where he come enter salisbury salisbury now by my sword well hast thou fought to day by the mass so did we all i thank you richard god know how long it is i have to live and it hath pleased him that three times to day you have defend me from imminent death well lords we have not got that which we have  tis not enough our foe are this time flee being opposite of such repair nature york i know our safety is to follow them for as i hear the king is flee to london to call a present court of parliament let us pursue him ere the writ go forth what say lord warwick shall we after them warwick after them nay before them if we can now by my faith lords twa a glorious day saint alban s battle won by famous york shall be eternize in all age to come sound drum and trumpet and to london all and more such days as these to us befall exeunt
