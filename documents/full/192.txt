scene ii france plain in anjou enter charles burgundy alencon bastard of orleans reignier joan la pucelle and forces charles these news my lord may cheer our droop spirits  tis said the stout parisian do revolt and turn again unto the warlike france alencon then march to paris royal charles of france and keep not back your powers in dalliance joan la pucelle peace be amongst them if they turn to us else ruin combat with their palace enter scout scout success unto our valiant general and happiness to his accomplice charles what tidings send our scout i prithee speak scout the english army that divide was into two party is now conjoin in one and means to give you battle presently charles somewhat too sudden sir the warning is but we will presently provide for them burgundy i trust the ghost of talbot is not there now he is gone my lord you need not fear joan la pucelle of all base passion fear is most accursed command the conquest charles it shall be thine let henry fret and all the world repine charles then on my lords and france be fortunate exeunt
